CLINICAL SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This comprehensive genomic profile was performed on tumor tissue from
a 58-year-old female with stage IIIB non-small cell lung cancer,
adenocarcinoma histology. The panel covers 324 cancer-relevant genes
including full exonic sequencing, select introns, and RNA fusion
analysis.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
GENOMIC FINDINGS — PATHOGENIC / LIKELY PATHOGENIC
----------------------------------------------------------------------

1. EGFR Exon 19 Deletion (p.E746_A750del)
   Classification: Pathogenic
   Allele Frequency: 42.3%
   The EGFR exon 19 deletion is the most common sensitizing mutation
   in NSCLC, present in approximately 45% of EGFR-mutant lung cancers.
   This alteration renders the tumor highly sensitive to EGFR tyrosine
   kinase inhibitors (TKIs) including osimertinib (Tagrisso), erlotinib,
   and gefitinib. First-line osimertinib is strongly recommended based
   on FLAURA trial data showing superior PFS (18.9 vs 10.2 months).

2. TP53 p.R175H (c.524G>A)
   Classification: Pathogenic
   Allele Frequency: 38.7%
   TP53 R175H is a well-characterized hotspot mutation resulting in loss
   of tumor suppressor function. Co-occurrence with EGFR mutations is
   common and may be associated with modest reduction in TKI response
   duration. No direct therapeutic implications but prognostically relevant.

3. PTEN p.R130* (c.388C>T) — Nonsense mutation
   Classification: Pathogenic
   Allele Frequency: 21.4%
   Loss-of-function mutation in PTEN leading to PI3K/AKT pathway
   activation. PTEN loss may contribute to resistance to EGFR-directed
   therapies and has been associated with worse outcomes in EGFR-mutant
   NSCLC. PI3K inhibitor combinations may be of interest in the
   relapsed/refractory setting.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
GENOMIC FINDINGS — VARIANTS OF UNCERTAIN SIGNIFICANCE (VUS)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

4. ATM p.V2424G (c.7271T>G)
   Classification: Variant of uncertain significance (VUS)
   Allele Frequency: 18.2%
   ATM encodes a serine/threonine kinase involved in DNA damage response.
   The clinical significance of this specific variant is currently
   uncertain. ATM loss-of-function has been associated with sensitivity
   to PARP inhibitors and ATR inhibitors in some tumor contexts, but
   insufficient evidence exists for this specific variant.

5. CHEK2 p.I157T (c.470T>C)
   Classification: Variant of uncertain significance (VUS)
   Allele Frequency: 49.1% (likely germline)
   CHEK2 I157T is a recurrent low-penetrance variant associated with
   moderately increased cancer risk. Germline confirmation recommended.
   Genetic counseling referral advised.

10. MET Amplification — High-level amplification (CN ~12)
    MET amplification is a known primary and acquired resistance

----------------------------------------------------------------------
METHODOLOGY
----------------------------------------------------------------------
DNA and RNA were extracted from FFPE tumor tissue. Hybrid-capture based
next-generation sequencing was performed on a 324-gene panel with mean
coverage depth of 650x. Variants with VAF ≥5% and coverage ≥100x were
reported. Tumor mutational burden calculated from 1.1 Mb of sequenced
coding genome. MSI assessed at 114 loci. HRD score derived from
genome-wide LOH, telomeric allelic imbalance, and large-scale
state transitions.

This report is intended for use by qualified healthcare professionals
and should be interpreted in the full clinical context. This is not
a standalone diagnostic report.
